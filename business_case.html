Business Case

The business case for a FHIR implementation guide to STI data is rooted in the potential benefits that standardized STI data exchange can bring to healthcare providers, payers, and other stakeholders. These benefits include improved patient outcomes, increased efficiency, and reduced costs.

The implementation of an STI FHIR IG can lead to improved patient outcomes. Standardized and consistent exchange of STI data can help healthcare providers to more accurately track and manage STI infections, ensuring that patients receive the appropriate care and treatment. This can help to improve patient health and reduce the risk of long-term complications associated with untreated STIs.

Standardized STI data exchange can also increase efficiency in healthcare delivery. By standardizing the way that STI data is collected, stored, and transmitted, healthcare providers can reduce the time and resources needed to manage STI infections. This can help enable providers to see more patients and deliver care more efficiently, improving their bottom line.

Finally, an STI FHIR IG can help to reduce costs. By improving patient outcomes and increasing efficiency, healthcare providers can reduce the costs associated with treating STIs. Additionally, standardized STI data exchange can help to reduce errors and inconsistencies in STI data, which can lead to costly rework and other issues.

Overall, the business case for an STI FHIR IG is driven by the potential benefits that it can bring to healthcare providers, payers, and other stakeholders. By improving patient outcomes, increasing efficiency, and reducing costs, a standardized implementation guide can help to improve the bottom line for healthcare organizations while also improving the health of their patients.
